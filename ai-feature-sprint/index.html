Your team knows AI matters. The hard part is shipping the right thing.

Most SaaS teams are already experimenting with AI. Someone has tried Claude Code. Someone has built a prototype. Someone has suggested adding a chatbot.

But useful AI features do not come from vague experimentation. They need a clear workflow, the right data, a narrow scope, product judgment, and an implementation path the team can actually ship.

This sprint is designed to help you move from "we should add AI" to one useful AI workflow your users or team can actually use.

Examples of narrow AI workflows

  • Customer summary generator. Turn scattered customer notes, usage data, and account history into a concise account summary.
  • Support ticket summarizer. Collapse long ticket threads into a clean status, root cause, and next action.
  • Sales email analyzer. Score inbound emails by intent, urgency, and fit, and surface the response worth writing.
  • Onboarding assistant. Guide new users through setup using their actual product context, not a generic tour.
  • Report generator. Produce weekly or monthly reports from product, sales, or finance data without copy-paste.
  • Investor update assistant. Draft a clean investor update from metrics, commentary, and key events.
  • Finance/runway scenario explainer. Translate a runway model into plain-language scenarios a founder can act on.
  • Internal admin assistant. Automate the small operational tasks that quietly burn your team's time every week.
  • Product analytics insight generator. Turn raw event data into a short list of insights worth a product decision.
  • Document extraction workflow. Pull structured fields out of contracts, invoices, or onboarding docs reliably.

How the 10-business-day sprint works

Day 1: Scope
We define the workflow, user problem, available data, constraints, and success criteria.
Days 2 to 3: Design
We map the UX flow, architecture, prompt and data structure, and implementation plan.
Days 4 to 5: Prototype
We build the first working version and validate the direction.
Days 6 to 9: Implementation
We refine the workflow, handle edge cases, add error states, improve prompts, and prepare for handoff or deployment.
Day 10: Handoff
You receive a staging deployment, production-ready pull request, or implementation handoff, depending on your infrastructure and access.
